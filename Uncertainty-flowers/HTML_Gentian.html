Gentian

Gentianella amarelle

Keywords

: Discouragement and despondency after setbacks.

Negative Indications

: Doubt, despondency, and easily discouraged by difficulty. Depression from known origin, gives up easily.

Positive Outcomes

: Conviction that difficulties will be overcome; failure is non-existent when doing one's best. Resilience in the face of setbacks; perseverance. Flexibility of outlook and broader perspective.

Clinical Applications and Uses

:

  • Gentian people are easily disheartened by minor setbacks at the level of their hopes and aspirations, particularly after hard work.
  • Despondency with long-term or recurrent illness, or children feeling discouraged about schoolwork. The failure to achieve a desired performance (in sport, work, relationships, etc.) leaves them feeling discouraged.
  • Ideas of how life should be if happiness is achieved are too set and not grounded in reality.

(Bach Center, 2007; Chancellor, 1995; Scheffer, 1988; Wells, 2002).
