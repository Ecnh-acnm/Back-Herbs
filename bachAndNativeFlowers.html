Bach flowers and Native flowers

Bach flowers and native flowers differ significantly in their origin and usage. Bach flowers refer to a system of flower remedies developed by Dr. Edward Bach in the 1930s, primarily used for emotional and psychological well-being. These remedies are created by infusing non-native flowers in water and are believed to help balance emotions. In contrast, native flowers are the indigenous flora of a specific region or country, naturally occurring without human intervention. They have ecological importance and often support local wildlife and pollinators. Native flowers are commonly used in landscaping and gardening to promote biodiversity and are appreciated for their aesthetic beauty. While Bach flowers focus on emotional healing, native flowers play a vital role in the ecosystem and local environment.
